The best GIF setting is a tradeoff: more frames, pixels, and seconds can improve detail, but each one increases file size. In YTgify v1.0.19, you can choose **144p, 240p, 360p, or 480p**, **5, 10, or 15 FPS**, and a clip from **1 to 20 seconds**.

The recommendations below are practical **YTgify starting points**, not official upload limits. Platforms change their limits, and workspaces or account tiers can differ.

## Quick settings by sharing goal

| Sharing goal | Resolution | FPS | Clip length | Why start here |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Lightweight chat reaction | 240p | 5 FPS | 2–3 sec | Prioritizes a small, quick-loading file. |
| Everyday social reaction | 360p | 10 FPS | 3–5 sec | A useful balance for motion and readable detail. |
| Fast motion or gesture | 360p | 15 FPS | 2–4 sec | More frames help motion, while short length controls size. |
| Text or interface detail | 480p | 10 FPS | 2–4 sec | More pixels help small visual details without maximizing FPS too. |
| Tiny sticker-like loop | 144p | 5 FPS | 1–2 sec | Uses the lowest-cost combination available. |

If a destination rejects the file, shorten it first. Duration usually gives you the biggest reduction without making each frame less legible.

## Resolution: choose only the detail you need

- **144p** is useful for tiny loops where detail is not important.
- **240p** suits compact chat reactions and simple expressions.
- **360p** is the best general starting point for faces, gestures, and most social sharing.
- **480p** preserves more text and interface detail, but costs more bytes per frame.

Higher resolution does not improve a blurry source. Choose the lowest setting that keeps the subject understandable at the size people will actually view it.

## Frame rate: spend frames on motion

- **5 FPS** keeps files small and works for slower reactions or sticker-like loops.
- **10 FPS** is a dependable default for ordinary movement.
- **15 FPS** looks smoother during quick gestures, camera movement, or action.

Increasing FPS from 5 to 15 roughly triples the number of captured frames for the same duration. Use 15 FPS when the motion benefits—not simply because it is the largest option.

## Duration: the strongest file-size control

YTgify v1.0.19 accepts a selection from 1 to 20 seconds, but the maximum is rarely the best sharing choice. Trim the setup and aftermath, and keep only the beat that makes the loop work.

A clean loop often follows this pattern:

1. Start just before the recognizable action or expression.
2. End as soon as that action resolves.
3. Preview the transition from the final frame back to the first.
4. Shorten again if a pause makes the loop feel sluggish.

## A simple tuning order

When the first export is too large, change one control at a time:

1. **Shorten duration.** Remove every fraction of a second that does not add meaning.
2. **Lower resolution.** Try 480p to 360p, or 360p to 240p.
3. **Lower FPS.** Try 15 to 10 FPS, then 10 to 5 FPS.
4. **Recheck legibility and motion.** Stop as soon as the GIF is clear enough for its destination.

This order preserves clarity better than immediately dropping every setting to its minimum.

## Three common mistakes

- **Treating recommendations as platform rules.** Check the destination's current upload message or help page when an exact limit matters.
- **Using 480p and 15 FPS for a long clip.** That combines all three file-size multipliers.
- **Leaving dead time around the moment.** Extra seconds add frames without improving the loop.
